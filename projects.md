# Projects

Below is a selection of projects that reflect my work in econometrics, impact evaluation, financial inclusion, and digital development.

---

## 📊 1. Impact Evaluation of Digital Financial Services
**Focus:** Assessing how mobile money and digital credit influence household welfare and microenterprise performance.  
**Methods:** Quasi-experimental designs, panel data econometrics, and robustness checks.  
**Status:** Ongoing  
**Highlights:**  
- Constructed a multi-year panel dataset  
- Implemented fixed-effects and IV estimators  
- Designed reproducible workflows for replication

---

## 🌐 2. Digital Connectivity and Urban Development
**Focus:** Exploring how broadband access affects productivity, employment, and city-level economic outcomes.  
**Methods:** Spatial econometrics, city-level indicators, and thematic mapping.  
**Status:** Completed  
**Highlights:**  
- Built a city-level connectivity index  
- Conducted spatial autocorrelation and hotspot analysis  
- Produced thematic maps for policy communication

---

## 🏦 3. Financial Inclusion and Household Resilience
**Focus:** Understanding how access to formal and informal finance shapes household resilience to shocks.  
**Methods:** Propensity score matching, treatment effects, and heterogeneity analysis.  
**Status:** In progress  
**Highlights:**  
- Developed a multidimensional financial inclusion index  
- Estimated heterogeneous treatment effects  
- Prepared policy-oriented summary tables

---

## 🧹 4. Reproducible SLR Workflow Using OpenRefine
**Focus:** Designing a minimal, transparent, and replicable workflow for systematic review data cleaning.  
**Methods:** GREL expressions, JSON pipelines, PRISMA reporting.  
**Status:** Completed  
**Highlights:**  
- Automated deduplication and normalization  
- Exported reproducible JSON scripts  
- Integrated with bibliometric mapping tools

---

## 📈 5. Econometric Modeling for Policy Evaluation
**Focus:** Applying econometric models to evaluate policy interventions in development and sustainability.  
**Methods:** Panel models, difference‑in‑differences, and causal inference.  
**Status:** Ongoing  
**Highlights:**  
- Built modular code for model diagnostics  
- Produced publication-ready tables and figures  
- Supported peer-reviewed manuscripts
